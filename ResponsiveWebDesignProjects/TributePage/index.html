Vladimir Putin

Prime Minister, President, Ruler of Russia since 2000.

A time line of Vladimir Putin's life:

  • October 1952 - born in Leningrad, Russian SFSR, Soviet Union (now Saint Petersburg)
  • September 1960 - started at School No. 193 at Baskov Lane, near his home
  • 1970 to 1975 - studied Law at the Leningrad State University (now Saint Petersburg State University)
  • 1975 - Putin joined the KGB and trained at the 401st KGB school in Okhta, Leningrad
  • September 1984 - was sent to Moscow for further training at the Yuri Andropov Red Banner Institute
  • 1985 to 1990 - served in Dresden, East Germany, using a cover identity as a translator
  • 1990 - returned to Leningrad, where he worked for about three months with the International Affairs section of Leningrad State University
  • 1991 - resigned from the KGB
  • 1994 to 1996 - held several other political and governmental positions in Saint Petersburg
  • 26 March 1997 - President Boris Yeltsin appointed Putin deputy chief of Presidential Staff
  • 25 May 1998 - was appointed First Deputy Chief of Presidential Staff for regions, replacing Viktoriya Mitina
  • 25 July 1998 - Yeltsin appointed Putin as Director of the Federal Security Service (FSB), the primary intelligence and security organization of the Russian Federation and the successor to the KGB
  • 9 August 1999 - was appointed acting Prime Minister of the Government of the Russian Federation by President Yeltsin
  • 31 December 1999 - Yeltsin unexpectedly resigned and, according to the Constitution of Russia, Putin became Acting President of the Russian Federation
  • 14 March 2004 - was elected to the presidency for a second term, receiving 71% of the vote
  • 2008 to 2012 - was barred from a third consecutive term by the Constitution. First Deputy Prime Minister Dmitry Medvedev was elected his successor. In a power-switching operation on 8 May 2008, only a day after handing the presidency to Medvedev, Putin was appointed Prime Minister of Russia, maintaining his political dominance
  • 4 March 2012 - won the 2012 Russian presidential elections in the first round, with 63.6% of the vote, despite widespread accusations of vote-rigging
  • 2018 - won the 2018 presidential election with more than 76% of the vote, which will last until 2024
  • 25 May 2018 - announced that he would not run for president in 2024, justifying this in compliance with the Russian Constitution
